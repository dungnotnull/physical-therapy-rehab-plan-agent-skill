## 1. Core Concepts & Frameworks

This skill reasons with the following world-renowned frameworks:
- **Tissue-healing phases (inflammatory/proliferative/remodeling)** - Three phases of soft tissue healing that guide exercise progression
- **Progressive overload & graded exposure** - Progressive loading principles for tissue adaptation
- **Clinical practice guidelines (APTA, JOSPT)** - Evidence-based practice guidelines from professional organizations
- **Red-flag screening (serious pathology)** - Systematic screening for serious medical conditions
- **Pain-monitoring (traffic-light) models** - Activity-based symptom monitoring systems
- **Outcome measures (e.g., LEFS, DASH, NPRS)** - Validated patient-reported outcome measures

**Scoring dimensions derived from these:** Recovery stage, Safety, Progression readiness, Pain control, Functional goal fit.

## 3. State-of-the-Art Methods & Tools

### Current Best Practice Frameworks

**Tissue Healing Timeline:**
- **Inflammatory phase (0-72 hours):** Protection, relative rest, pain management
- **Proliferative phase (72h-6 weeks):** Gradual loading, collagen synthesis
- **Remodeling phase (6+ weeks):** Progressive strengthening, sport-specific drills

**Load Management Principles:**
- **Mechanotransduction:** Mechanical loading drives tissue adaptation
- **Optimal loading window:** Load must be adequate to stimulate adaptation without causing damage
- **Progressive overload:** Systematic increase of load variables

**Pain Monitoring Systems:**
- **Traffic light model:** Green (safe to continue), Yellow (modify activity), Red (stop and seek care)
- **Pain provocation:** Symptoms during activity vs. after activity
- **24-hour rule:** Pain should not increase 24 hours post-activity

**Return to Sport Criteria:**
- **Physical criteria:** Strength (>90% LLI), power, agility, sport-specific movements
- **Psychological readiness:** ACL-RSI, fear-avoidance measures
- **Time-based vs. criteria-based:** Individualized progression over fixed timelines

### Evidence Hierarchy
**Systematic Review > Meta-Analysis > RCT/empirical > Cohort > Expert Opinion > Blog/website**

## 8. Clinical Reasoning Rules

### When to Refer Immediately
- Suspected fracture (deformity, inability to bear weight, bony tenderness)
- Infection signs (fever, warmth, erythema, elevated inflammatory markers)
- Deep vein thrombosis (unilateral swelling, warmth, recent surgery)
- Neurological deficits (loss of sensation, motor weakness, bowel/bladder changes)
- Systemic signs of serious pathology (unexplained weight loss, night pain)

### When to Require Medical Clearance
- Failure to progress with appropriate care
- Red flags present but not emergent
- Diagnostic imaging required for progression
- Surgical considerations
- Comorbidities affecting healing

### Safe Progression Principles
1. Never increase more than one variable at a time (load, volume, frequency)
2. Monitor response to each progression step
3. Use pain provocation rules to determine appropriate load
4. Consider tissue healing stage and tissue type
5. Address impairments before returning to high-level activity

### Pain-Based Activity Rules
**Continue activity if:**
- Pain is ≤3/10 during activity
- Pain returns to baseline within 24 hours
- No loss of function next day
- No compensation patterns

**Modify activity if:**
- Pain is 4-6/10 during activity
- Pain increases above baseline next day
- Noticeable compensation develops

**Stop activity if:**
- Pain is ≥7/10
- Sharp, shooting, or radiating pain
- Immediate loss of function
- Feeling of instability

## 10. Limitations and Boundaries

### Scope Limitations
- Educational purposes only, not a substitute for in-person physical therapy
- Cannot provide physical examination or hands-on assessment
- Limited to peer-reviewed literature (may miss cutting-edge interventions)
- Language and cultural factors may affect applicability

### Knowledge Gaps
- Pediatric considerations (where not specifically cited)
- Rare conditions with limited evidence
- Emerging technologies (e.g., wearable sensors, AI-driven rehab)
- Complementary and alternative medicine evidence

### Critical Thinking Reminders
- Individual response to intervention varies
- Context factors (therapeutic alliance, expectations) affect outcomes
- Evidence gaps require transparent acknowledgment
- When in doubt, conservative progression is safest
